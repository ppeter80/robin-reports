## TL;DR

**Patient long.** FY25 was a wipe-out — operating profit halved to **€8.9 bn (-53%)**, net income **€6.9 bn (-44%)**, margin **2.8% reported / 4.6% adjusted** ex **€5.9 bn of US-tariff and Porsche specials**. Dividend cut 17% to **€5.26/pref**. **But:** the franchise is intact — revenue flat at **€321.9 bn**, automotive net cash flow **+24% to €6.4 bn**, deliveries held at **9.0 m units** (BEV +32%), automotive net liquidity **€34.5 bn**.

**FY26 guide implies recovery** — margin **4.0–5.5%**, NCF **€3–6 bn**. Stock has already absorbed most of the shock (-12.6% YTD).

**Probability-weighted FV ≈ €100–115 (+13% to +30%)**. Asymmetry favours buyers on a 2-3 yr view, but bear case isn't zero.

## What dragged FY25 down

**€2.9 bn** US-tariff cash hit. **€4.7 bn** Porsche-related charges (model strategy reset, EV ramp deferral, goodwill review). Plus residual restructuring across Audi/TRATON. Together: **€5.9 bn of special effects**. Adjusting them out gives a **4.6% operating margin** — basically the midpoint of the FY26 guide. The reset is happening; the question is execution.

## Segments — FY25 highlights

- **Core (VW/Skoda/SEAT/CUPRA)** — Rev **€145.2 bn (+3.7%)**, op margin **4.7%**. The bright spot. Cost programme on track, mix improving, EU order intake **+13% YoY**.
- **Sport Luxury (Porsche AG)** — Rev **€32.2 bn (-11.7%)**, op margin **0.3%**. The pain trade. Strategy reset still working through.
- **Progressive (Audi)** — restructuring overhang, new platform transitions through 2027.
- **Trucks (TRATON)** — soft freight cycle but profitable.
- **Financial Services** — stable cash earner.

*Q4'25 alone:* Rev **€83.2 bn (-4.7%)**, op profit **€3.46 bn**, margin **4.2%**, EPS **€3.39** (vs €5.99 cons, -43% miss — but this includes the Porsche charge concentration).

## FY2026 guidance and Q1'26 read

Management guides **revenue 0–3%**, **op margin 4.0–5.5%**, **auto NCF €3–6 bn**. Q1'26 deliveries **-4% YoY**, but order intake **+3%** — backlog is rebuilding. **30 April 2026 Q1 release** is the next datapoint: if margin prints >4.5% with tariff cost absorbed, base case strengthens; below 3.5%, bear scenario becomes live.

## Key risks

- **Tariffs** — US 25% on EU autos; €2.9 bn FY25 hit. Trade-deal optionality, but path uncertain.
- **China** — share losses to local OEMs (BYD, Xiaomi, Leapmotor); JV restructuring drag continues.
- **Porsche AG** — €4.7 bn charges in FY25; further model strategy / EV resets possible.
- **Audi** — restructuring overhang; new platform transitions through 2027.
- **BEV economics** — gross margin still negative; dependent on SSP/PPE platform scale.
- **Capital allocation** — €34.5 bn auto net liquidity is strong, but dividend cut signals stress.
- **Regulation** — EU CO2 fleet targets; potential €/unit fines if mix shifts slip.

## Catalysts to watch

- **30 Apr 2026** — Q1 financial release. Watch: margin trajectory, US tariff absorption, Porsche guidance.
- **Jun 2026** — AGM, dividend approval (€5.26 pref proposed).
- **H2 2026** — SSP platform milestones (ID.2 / Trinity).
- TRATON margin durability under freight-cycle softness.
- Any **US-EU tariff resolution** = direct earnings catalyst (~€2-3 bn annualised).

## Stance

**Patient long, sized small.** The reset is real but not finished. **€88.74 is closer to the bear anchor than the base**, which gives an asymmetric setup if you can hold through another quarter or two of noise. The dividend cut is the right signal — management is admitting the year is broken and preserving balance sheet rather than papering over. The €34.5 bn auto net liquidity means they don't have to do anything stupid. **Add on weakness, trim on a clean Q1'26 print and tariff resolution.** Sell-side mean PT around €100 is anchored on FY26 guide; my range gives more weight to FY27-28 recovery.
